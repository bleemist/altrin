Our Flagship Initiatives

Seven interconnected programs designed to create lasting impact across education, economic empowerment, and environmental sustainability.

Program 01

Learner Tracking Systems

From P.1 Enrolment to University Graduation

The Learner Tracking System is a comprehensive monitoring framework that follows each child's academic journey from the moment they enroll in Primary 1 all the way through to university graduation. This system is designed to minimize school dropout rates and improve graduation rates by tracking academic progress, attendance patterns, and at-risk indicators across key transition years.

Key Components:

  • Academic performance tracking across all subjects
  • Attendance monitoring and early warning systems
  • Transition support between primary, secondary, and tertiary levels
  • Identification of at-risk learners for targeted intervention

Outcome: 500+ learners currently being tracked across 10+ partner schools in Buliisa District.

Methodology

1

Enrolment & Baseline

Each learner is registered with a unique identifier, capturing their demographic and academic baseline data.

2

Continuous Monitoring

Termly assessments, attendance records, and teacher feedback are collected and analyzed.

3

Intervention & Support

At-risk learners receive targeted support through tutoring, counseling, or parent engagement.

4

Transition & Graduation

Learners are supported through key transition points — P.7 to S.1, S.4 to S.5, and S.6 to university.

Program 02

Career Guidance & Counselling

Academic counselling that accelerates ambitions, helping secondary students make informed career choices through aptitude assessments, career exploration, and role-model sessions with professionals from various fields.

Program Features:

  • Career aptitude assessments and personality profiling
  • Career exploration workshops and industry exposure visits
  • Role-model sessions with professionals in diverse fields
  • Post-secondary planning and scholarship guidance

Outcome: 15+ schools have benefited from career guidance sessions, reaching 300+ secondary students annually.

Methodology

1

Assessment

Students complete aptitude tests and career interest inventories to identify strengths and preferences.

2

Exploration

Students explore various career paths through workshops, guest speakers, and industry visits.

3

Mentorship

Students are matched with mentors from their fields of interest for ongoing guidance and support.

Program 03

Teaching at the Right Level (TaRL)

A proven pedagogical approach that groups learners by ability rather than age, improving foundational literacy and numeracy for targeted impact. TaRL is designed to address learning gaps in under-resourced schools where students often fall behind grade-level expectations.

Key Features:

  • Ability-based grouping rather than age-based
  • Focus on foundational literacy and numeracy skills
  • Regular assessment and regrouping based on progress
  • Teacher training and ongoing coaching support

Outcome: 4,200+ learners reached in 2025 through TaRL interventions, with significant improvements in literacy and numeracy scores.

Methodology

1

Assessment

Learners are assessed to determine their current literacy and numeracy levels, not their grade level.

2

Grouping

Learners are grouped by ability level, allowing teachers to target instruction to specific learning needs.

3

Instruction

Teachers deliver targeted, activity-based instruction designed for each ability group.

4

Reassessment

Regular reassessment ensures learners are regrouped as they progress, celebrating growth and identifying new needs.

Program 04

Mentorship Program

Connecting students to professionals through mentor-mentee relationships, career workshops, internships, and post-graduate placements. The mentorship program builds bridges between education and employment, helping young people navigate their career journeys with confidence.

Program Components:

  • One-on-one mentor-mentee matching based on career interests
  • Career workshops and professional development sessions
  • Internship and job shadowing opportunities
  • Post-graduate placement support and alumni network

Outcome: 15+ partner schools participating in mentorship programs, with 100+ active mentor-mentee relationships.

Methodology

1

Matching

Mentors and mentees are carefully matched based on career interests, goals, and personality.

2

Engagement

Regular mentor-mentee meetings, workshops, and career development activities.

3

Evaluation

Ongoing assessment of the mentorship impact on students' academic and career outcomes.

Program 05

Parents & Teachers Capacity Building

Teacher workshops in modern teaching techniques combined with income-generating projects for parents to meet their children's educational needs. This program recognizes that educational success requires support from both the classroom and the home.

Key Components:

  • Teacher training in modern teaching methodologies
  • Parent workshops on financial literacy and small business management
  • Income-generating project incubation and support
  • Parent cooperatives and village savings and loan associations (VSLAs)

Outcome: 20+ teachers trained in modern teaching techniques, 15+ parent income-generating projects initiated, and 3 parent cooperatives formed.

Methodology

1

Training

Teachers participate in intensive workshops on TaRL, learner-centered methods, and classroom management.

2

Empowerment

Parents receive training in financial literacy, business planning, and cooperative management.

3

Sustain

Ongoing mentoring and support for teachers and parents to ensure long-term sustainability and impact.

Program 06

Environmental Stewardship & Climate Action

Integrating climate education, tree planting initiatives, and sustainable practices into our community programs. We believe that a healthy environment is foundational to thriving communities and educational success.

Key Components:

  • Climate education integrated into school curricula
  • Tree planting and reforestation campaigns
  • Sustainable farming and environmental conservation practices
  • Community awareness and advocacy for climate action

Outcome: 500+ trees planted across partner schools and communities, 10+ schools with climate education programs, and growing community awareness on environmental issues.

Methodology

1

Education

Climate education integrated into school activities and community outreach programs.

2

Action

Tree planting campaigns, clean-up drives, and sustainable practice demonstrations.

3

Advocacy

Community engagement and advocacy for environmental conservation and climate action.

Program 07

Academic Competitions

Debates, poem & essay writing to sharpen presentation, public speaking, and formal argument skills — unlocking confidence beyond textbooks. These competitions create platforms for students to showcase their talents and develop essential life skills.

Key Components:

  • Inter-school debating competitions
  • Poem and essay writing contests
  • Public speaking and presentation workshops
  • Recognition and awards for outstanding participants

Outcome: Annual Inter-Secondary Schools Debate & Poem Launch, with hundreds of students participating each year.

Methodology

1

Preparation

Students receive training in debate, public speaking, and writing skills through workshops and coaching.

2

Competition

Inter-school competitions provide platforms for students to showcase their skills and learn from peers.

3

Recognition

Awards and certificates recognize excellence, motivating continued participation and skill development.
